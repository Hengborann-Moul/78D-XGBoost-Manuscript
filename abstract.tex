\begin{mdframed}[
    linewidth=0.4pt,
    linecolor=povteal,

    innerleftmargin=5pt,

  \noindent\textbf{Abstract:}
  \textit{
    %--------------------------------------------------
    % Context: General
    Khmer Mathematical Word Problems (KMWP) present a significant challenge in natural language
    processing due to the low-resource nature of the Khmer language and the near-complete absence
    of publicly available benchmark datasets.
    %-------------------------------------------------
    % Data: Source, Size, What is it about?
    In this study, we construct a dataset of $8,000$
    primary school-level KMWP samples sourced from the official Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport
    (MOEYS) materials, each paired with step-by-step solution guidance.
    %-------------------------------------------------
    %    Methodology:
    %        - Key algorithm
    %        - Special Method
    %        - Objective
    We fine-tune the
    Qwen2.5-1.5B large language model using the Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) framework.
    %---------------------------------------------------
    % Summary of the result
    The model achieves ROUGE-L score of $0.8809$, a cosine similarity of $0.9631$, and a numerical
    accuracy of $81.00\%$ on $400$ test samples. These results represent a dramatic improvement over the base model, which attains $0.00\%$ accuracy in zero-shot settings and only $4.50\%$ in one-shot settings.
    %---------------------------------------------
    %    Closing:
    %    - First Work
    %    - Improve...
    %    - A remark on our achievement
    %    - Future: open more direction for future research.
    This work not only produces a KMWP for future research in NLP, but also demonstrates that targeted fine-tuning with Chain-of-Thought
    Supervision is an effective strategy for enabling AI models to reason through mathematical
    word problems in a low-resource language. The study supports the United Nations Sustainable
    Development Goals of Quality Education (SDG 4), Industry and Innovation (SDG 9), and Reduced
    Inequalities (SDG 10) by providing an AI-powered learning tool for Khmer-speaking students.
  }
\end{mdframed}
